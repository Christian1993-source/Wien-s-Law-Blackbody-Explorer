Blackbody Radiation

Laboratory Notes

A blackbody is an ideal object that emits electromagnetic radiation determined only by its temperature.

Examples include:

  • stars
  • planets
  • hot metals
  • incandescent filaments

Students enter an object's temperature and analyze its radiation using Wien's Law.

Peak Wavelength (Wien's Law)

Formula Card
$$\lambda_{\text{max}} = \frac{0.0029}{T}$$
λmax = peak wavelength T = temperature (Kelvin)
